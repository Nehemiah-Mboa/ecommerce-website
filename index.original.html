The Premium Nature of Variegation

Variegation is a rare genetic mutation that limits a plant's ability to produce chlorophyll in specific sectors of its leaves and petals. This lack of green pigment results in visually breathtaking patterns of stark white, cream, and warm gold.

Because these variegated areas do not photosynthesize, the plants grow significantly slower and require specialized, precise care. It is this slower growth, combined with unstable genetics and overwhelming global demand, that makes these specimens true botanical investments.
